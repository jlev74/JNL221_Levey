Hip hop vocabulary

The data for the project comes from listening to different hip hop rappers and their lyrics to pick out how many unique words each of them have. The first dataviz element in the article is an intereactive scatterplot that has every artist's face and it says their name, and the number of words that they have in their first 35,000 words. The second dataviz is a stacked column chart with the hip hop artist's highlighted in a different color to differentiate by era. And the third piece of dataviz is an area column chart that compares unique lyrics in Country, Rock and Hip Hop songs.

The key message of the dataviz project is to display the creativity of the Hip Hop genre. Within the genre, artists can use pretty much any assortment of words, as well as make up their own. One way that this dataviz is effective is putting a face to how many unique words are used. By showing who the artists is, viewers can get a better look at who uses the most unique words. The stacked column chart is also effective because separating the artists into their eras helps visualize when using more unique words started growing in usage. And the third dataviz chart is effective because with showing the overlay of unique word usage throughout different genres shows how unique Hip Hop really is.

The dataviz is visceral because looking at the interactive scatter plot with the artists' faces on it and the stacked column having all the names, fans of each artists can see where they ranked against other artists. Being able to see and find their own favorite artist is more personal to people because seeing names not super memorable, but being able to see every artist's face, is more memorable and unique to look at. People in general have a deeper connection to pictures than they do written out names.
